## Summary

Replace the current `saw_on: bool` and `pulse_on: bool` flags on
`VDcoMix` with continuous gains `saw_gain: f32` and `pulse_gain: f32`
in `[0.0, 1.0]`. Add a third phase-locked waveform — triangle —
generated by wavefolding the saw (the Jupiter trick: full-wave rectify
a bipolar saw to produce a triangle at 2× frequency, or half-wave
fold for triangle at fundamental). Triangle has its own
`triangle_gain: f32 ∈ [0.0, 1.0]`.

Existing Juno-style patches still produce identical output by setting
gains to `0.0` or `1.0`; the parameter space is a strict superset of
the boolean version.

## Acceptance criteria

- [ ] `VDcoMix` changes:
      - `saw_on: bool` → `saw_gain: f32` (default `1.0`)
      - `pulse_on: bool` → `pulse_gain: f32` (default `0.0`)
      - new `triangle_gain: f32` (default `0.0`)
      - `sub_level` and `noise_level` unchanged.
- [ ] `render_and_advance` scales each waveform's contribution by its
      gain before summing. PolyBLEP corrections scale with the same
      gain (so the BLEP-to-raw ratio is preserved at any gain).
- [ ] Triangle generation: wavefold the raw saw. Pick the fold shape
      explicitly — recommend bipolar saw → `tri = 1 - 2*|2*phase - 1|`
      (the standard absolute-value triangle at the fundamental,
      not 2×). Document the choice in the module doc. No separate
      BLEP needed (triangle's corners are C⁰, not discontinuities);
      consider BLAMP at `phase = 0.5` if residual aliasing bothers.
- [ ] Parameters expose `saw_gain`, `pulse_gain`, `triangle_gain`
      with float type and `[0, 1]` range on both `VDco` and
      `VPolyDco`.
- [ ] Module doc comment (`core.rs` header and/or struct docs)
      updated to show the three-waveform mix plus sub and noise.
- [ ] DSL/patch migration: any existing `.patches` fixtures or demo
      patches referencing `saw_on` / `pulse_on` by name are updated
      to `saw_gain = 1.0` / `pulse_gain = 1.0`. If the DSL surface
      uses parameter names directly, the change is breaking; note
      in PR description.
- [ ] Tests: each gain at `0.0` silences that waveform (no BLEP
      leakage); gain `0.5` produces exactly half amplitude; triangle
      output alone is continuous and symmetric; all three summed at
      `1.0` is audible and stable.

## Notes

Triangle-from-folded-saw keeps the phase lock to saw/pulse/sub
absolute — a single phasor still drives everything, which is the
whole point of the Juno/Jupiter DCO architecture. Adding a separate
triangle oscillator would break that.

BLAMP refinement (band-limited corner for triangle) can be a
follow-up if aliasing on the triangle is audible; initial
implementation uses the naive wavefold since its harmonic content
falls off 6 dB/oct faster than saw and is usually inaudible.

Interacts with 0638 (softness): triangle follows phase just like saw,
so slewed reset rounds triangle's corners naturally.
